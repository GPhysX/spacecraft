3 /* Converts struct tm to time_t, assuming the data in tm is UTC rather
4  than local timezone.
5 
6  mktime is similar but assumes struct tm, also known as the
7  "broken-down" form of time, is in local time zone. mktime_from_utc
8  uses mktime to make the conversion understanding that an offset
9  will be introduced by the local time assumption.
10 
11  mktime_from_utc then measures the introduced offset by applying
12  gmtime to the initial result and applying mktime to the resulting
13  "broken-down" form. The difference between the two mktime results
14  is the measured offset which is then subtracted from the initial
15  mktime result to yield a calendar time which is the value returned.
16 
17  tm_isdst in struct tm is set to 0 to force mktime to introduce a
18  consistent offset (the non DST offset) since tm and tm+o might be
19  on opposite sides of a DST change.
20 
21  Some implementations of mktime return -1 for the nonexistent
22  localtime hour at the beginning of DST. In this event, use
23  mktime(tm - 1hr) + 3600.
24 
25  Schematically
26  mktime(tm) --> t+o
27  gmtime(t+o) --> tm+o
28  mktime(tm+o) --> t+2o
29  t+o - (t+2o - t+o) = t
30 
31  Note that glibc contains a function of the same purpose named
32  `timegm' (reverse of gmtime). But obviously, it is not universally
33  available, and unfortunately it is not straightforwardly
34  extractable for use here. Perhaps configure should detect timegm
35  and use it where available.
36 
37  Contributed by Roger Beeman <beeman@cisco.com>, with the help of
38  Mark Baushke <mdb@cisco.com> and the rest of the Gurus at CISCO.
39  Further improved by Roger with assistance from Edward J. Sabol
40  based on input by Jamie Zawinski. */
